# Movement Hermeneutics and the *Ma* 間 Framework — Note for classics-124-poetics

*Source: Author interview with Vivien Jiaqian Zhu, Eliva Press, on* Exploring Art, Knowledge and Movement in Japanese Fashion *(2025).*

## Why this belongs in the comps repo

The repo's throughline argument tracks how defenses of transgressive representation — across Western classical poetics and Ming/Qing *Jin Ping Mei* commentary alike — locate their justification in an external interpretive frame that *produces*, rather than merely describes, the moral and gender categories it claims to explain. The interview material on the Japanese-fashion book gives a second, non-literary instance of the same structural move, which is useful as a comparative data point or a methodological appendix.

## The parallel

- **The frame in question**: "movement hermeneutics," developed to read avant-garde garment construction (e.g. Rei Kawakubo) as encoding classical Japanese spatial concepts (*ma* 間) that are largely absent from the textual record.
- **The constitutive claim**: garments are treated as "physical archives" transmitting somatic memory — i.e., the interpretive frame doesn't just describe the garment, it retroactively assigns it a historical/textual lineage (early modern Japanese palaeography, court drama records) that the frame itself supplies.
- **Structural echo with the *Jin Ping Mei* case**: just as Zhang Zhupo's commentary (working from Recension B) supplies a moral-didactic frame that produces the categories it claims only to explain in the transgressive body it reads, "movement hermeneutics" supplies a historical-continuity frame that produces the archival status of the garment it reads. Both cases externalize the justificatory work onto a frame that claims descriptive neutrality while doing constitutive work.

## Possible use in the repo

- As a short comparative sidebar in the theoretical-architecture section (alongside `mimesis_to_performativity.md`), extending the four-way comparison (Plato/Aristotle/Longinus/Butler) with a fifth, non-Western-classical instance of frame-as-producer.
- As a methodological citation for how "reading the body through an external interpretive apparatus" recurs outside the Ming-Qing/Butler axis — useful if a committee member asks whether the argument generalizes beyond the two core traditions.
