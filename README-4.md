# classics-124-poetics

Comparative materials tracing a throughline from Western classical poetics to the *Jin Ping Mei* (金瓶梅) commentarial tradition, read through Judith Butler's performativity framework.

## Project overview

This repository assembles working notes and source comparisons for a research project connecting:

- **Western classical poetics**: defenses of transgressive representation in Plato, Aristotle, Longinus, and Sidney
- **Jin Ping Mei commentarial tradition**: Zhang Zhupo's 1695 Xunzian commentary and the earlier Chongzhen commentary
- **Contemporary theory**: Judith Butler's account of gender performativity

A key textual finding anchors the comparative work: David Roy's Princeton translation of *Jin Ping Mei* is based on Recension A (*cihua*, 1610), while Zhang Zhupo and the Chongzhen commentator worked from Recension B (*xiuxiang*). This means Roy's English edition imports Zhang's interpretive frame onto a textual base Zhang was not actually commenting on — a mismatch with consequences for how the novel's commentarial tradition gets read in English-language scholarship.

## Structure

Working materials are staged across source-comparison files, organized roughly as:

- Primary source excerpts and translations (Plato, Aristotle, Longinus, Sidney)
- Zhang Zhupo and Chongzhen commentary notes, keyed to recension
- Butler performativity framework notes and points of contact
- Secondary scholarship notes (Naifei Ding's *Obscene Things*, and related work)
