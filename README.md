# classics-124-poetics

Notes and comps-prep material built around Berkeley Classics 124: *Classical Poetics* (G.R.F. Ferrari, Spring 2016) — used here as a theoretical foundation list for comprehensive exam preparation, with connections drawn out to my own research on gender performativity and body representation in classical Chinese literature and drama.

## Scope

The course syllabus runs chronologically from Plato and Aristotle through Longinus, late antiquity (Plotinus, Plutarch, Augustine), Sidney, and into early modern/Enlightenment poetics (Corneille, Pope, Hume, Lessing). Notes here are organized thematically rather than by week, grouped around recurring theoretical problems:

- **Mimesis and fictionality** — Plato (*Republic*, *Phaedrus*), Aristotle (*Poetics*), Sidney (*Defence of Poetry*)
- **Poetry's moral and political effects** — *Republic* 10, *Politics* 8, Plutarch, Sidney's refutation of the "nurse of abuse" charge
- **Beauty, eros, and desire** — *Phaedrus*, *Symposium*, Plotinus's *Enneads*
- **Comparative pressure points** — places where this Western tradition's defenses of transgressive/mimetic content can be set against non-Western commentarial traditions, especially the Ming/Qing *Jin Ping Mei* commentarial apparatus (Zhang Zhupo, the Chongzhen commentator, Naifei Ding's critique)
